Scoring and Banding

Each category is scored 1–4 by your Manager. Total possible: 20. Bands describe the overall result; category-level minimums protect against a strong total masking a weak category.

TotalBandWhat It Means
17–20MasteryYou can independently apply this strategy at advisory-quality. Pass.
14–16ProficientYou have solid command of the framework. Specific gaps identified for self-directed follow-up. Pass.
11–13DevelopingMaterial gaps. Conditional pass: your Manager assigns a targeted re-work (not a full re-presentation) due before Week 5 starts.
≤ 10Below BarDid not meet the bar. You re-present the topic in the following week alongside the new week's deliverables.

Category-level rule: any single category scored 1 results in conditional pass at minimum, regardless of total. Two or more categories scored 1 = Below Bar regardless of total.

Detailed Descriptors

1. Technical Accuracy

Rules under §121, §1031, §1245, §1250, §469(g), §1402(a)(1), §311(b), §6654, and Rev. Proc. 2019-38 stated correctly?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4MasteryAll rules stated correctly. §121 ownership/use, §1031 mechanics, §1245 vs §1250 split, §469(g) suspended loss release, §1402(a)(1), §311(b), §6654 safe harbor, and Rev. Proc. 2019-38 all applied precisely. State conformity rules cited correctly.
3ProficientRules substantially correct. Minor doctrinal slips that do not change conclusions.
2DevelopingMaterial errors (confused §1245 with §1250, missed §469(g) release on disposition, mis-stated reasonable comp framework). Some conclusions wrong as a result.
1Below BarFoundational rule errors. Conclusions wrong.

2. Authoritative Sourcing

Every claim tied to authoritative guidance with proper citation?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4MasteryEvery claim tied to specific IRC / Reg / Rev. Proc. / IRS guidance citation. Binding distinguished from secondary. State conformity sourced to statute or DOR guidance. HARPTA position confirmed against current Hawaii DOR.
3ProficientCitations present but with one or two gaps (e.g., relying on a tax-firm blog instead of pulling the underlying Reg).
2DevelopingCitations weak or generic. Multiple claims uncited.
1Below BarSubstantial unsourced assertions. Cannot defend if challenged.

3. Calculation Accuracy & Sensitivity

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4MasterySale math complete and correct: amount realized, basis recomputation, §1245 vs §1250 split, suspended PAL timing, federal plus state. 1031 boot and basis carryover correct. S-Corp savings math defensible. Sensitivity shown — what changes if cost seg percentage, sale price, or state assumption changes.
3ProficientMath substantially correct. Sensitivity discussion thin.
2DevelopingMaterial calculation errors. No sensitivity analysis.

4. Coverage & Risk Identification

3ProficientMost scenarios and risks covered. One or two gaps.
2DevelopingMultiple scenarios or risks missed. State layer thin or absent.
