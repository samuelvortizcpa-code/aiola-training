Week 2 Presentation Scorecard

Days 8–14 · STR (Wed) and REPS (Fri) Strategy Framework Presentations

Purpose
Objective rubric used by your Manager to evaluate both Week 2 presentations. The same five categories apply to both Wednesday's STR presentation and Friday's REPS presentation. You receive this scorecard at the start of the week so the bar is clear from day one and you can pressure-test your own work against it before delivering.

The Five Categories at a Glance

#CategoryWhat It Measures
1Technical AccuracyAre the rules, tests, thresholds, and exceptions stated correctly? Are common doctrinal traps avoided?
2Authoritative SourcingIs every claim tied to specific authority — IRC, Treasury Regs, IRS guidance, case law — with section-level (or better) citations? Is binding authority distinguished from secondary sources?
3Coverage & CompletenessAre all key elements addressed: definitions, qualifications, every relevant test, exceptions, gray areas, audit defensibility?
4Application to Real Client FactsIs the framework cleanly applied to the assigned client's actual facts? Are gray areas, risks, and missing-info items named with what it would take to resolve them?
5Communication & DefensibilityCould a peer follow it from the deck alone? Does it hold up under Q&A? Could the substance (with tone adjustment) be delivered to a client?

Scoring and Banding

Each category is scored 1–4 by your Manager. Total possible: 20. Bands describe the overall result; category-level minimums protect against a strong total masking a weak category.

TotalBandWhat It Means
17–20MasteryYou can independently apply this strategy at advisory-quality. Pass.
14–16ProficientYou have solid command of the framework. Specific gaps identified for self-directed follow-up. Pass.
11–13DevelopingMaterial gaps. Conditional pass: your Manager assigns a targeted re-work (not a full re-presentation) due before Week 3 starts.
≤ 10Below BarDid not meet the bar. You re-present the topic in the following week alongside the new week's deliverables.

Category-level rule: any single category scored 1 results in conditional pass at minimum, regardless of total. Two or more categories scored 1 = Below Bar regardless of total.

Detailed Descriptors

1. Technical Accuracy

Are the rules, tests, thresholds, and exceptions stated correctly?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4ExceedsAll rules stated correctly with precision. Tests applied with appropriate scope (e.g., distinguishes "rental real estate activity" from "real property trade or business"). Proactively addresses common doctrinal traps (e.g., 7-day STR exception removes activity from §469 rental classification, not from passive treatment automatically; §469(c)(7) is a status election, not an aggregation election).
3MeetsAll core rules stated correctly. Tests applied correctly. Minor imprecision permissible if not material to the conclusion.
2ApproachingMost rules correct, but at least one material error or significant imprecision (e.g., misstates a threshold, conflates two tests, misapplies an exception).
1BelowMultiple doctrinal errors that would lead to incorrect client advice if delivered as-is.

2. Authoritative Sourcing

Is every claim tied to authoritative guidance with proper citation?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4ExceedsEvery substantive claim tied to specific authority with sub-section-level citation (e.g., Reg §1.469-1T(e)(3)(ii)(A), not just "the regs"). Distinguishes binding authority (Code, Regs) from sub-regulatory guidance (revenue rulings, IRS pubs) appropriately. Case law cited with full case name, citation, and holding.
3MeetsAll substantive claims tied to authority at section level. Authoritative vs secondary distinction maintained. Cases cited correctly with name and holding.
2ApproachingMost claims sourced, but at least one significant gap: an uncited substantive claim, a blog post or secondary source standing in for primary authority, or vague citation ("the regulations say…").
1BelowMultiple uncited substantive claims; reliance on secondary sources for binding positions; case law absent or miscited.

3. Coverage & Completeness

Are all key elements addressed at appropriate depth?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4ExceedsAll key elements addressed (definitions, qualifications, every relevant test, exceptions, gray areas, audit defensibility) plus non-obvious considerations called out (e.g., spousal hour rules, NIIT/QBI interaction, aggregation election binding effect, contemporaneous log requirement, audit-trigger profile).
3MeetsAll key elements addressed at solid depth. Gray areas identified. Audit defensibility addressed.
2ApproachingOne or two key elements missed or addressed too superficially (e.g., gray areas not surfaced, no audit-defensibility discussion, an exception glossed over).
1BelowMultiple key elements missing or surface-level. Presentation feels like an outline rather than a framework.

4. Application to Real Client Facts

Is the framework cleanly applied to the assigned client's actual facts?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4ExceedsFramework cleanly applied to the assigned client. Specific facts pulled from the file (return, ISM, questionnaire) drive the analysis. Gray areas and risks named with what would resolve them. Identifies missing information needed to firm up the conclusion.
3MeetsFramework applied to the assigned client. Key facts identified and used. Gray areas named.
2ApproachingApplication is generic — client facts mentioned but not really driving the analysis. Could substitute any client and the slides wouldn't change much.
1BelowFramework presented in the abstract; little or no real connection to the assigned client's facts.

5. Communication & Defensibility

Could a peer follow it from the deck alone, and does it hold up under Q&A?

ScoreLevelDescriptor
4ExceedsLogical structure, clearly signposted. A peer could reproduce the conclusion from the slides without the talk track. Holds up under hostile Q&A; can defend gray-area positions on authority. Substance could be delivered to a client (with tone adjustment) without re-prepping.
3MeetsClear structure. Peer can follow it. Q&A handled professionally with citations to back positions.
2ApproachingStructure adequate, but follow-up questions reveal weak spots; some positions stated without ready ability to defend.
1BelowHard to follow; Q&A reveals gaps in understanding; can't defend conclusions when challenged.

How Feedback Is Delivered

WhenWhat Happens
During the sessionYour Manager listens, asks clarifying questions and pushback in Q&A, takes notes against the rubric. No score discussion mid-session.
Immediately afterYour Manager gives a 5-minute live read: top strengths, top gaps, headline band. No category-by-category breakdown yet — you hear the verdict and the headline.
Same dayYour Manager completes and shares the filled scoring sheet. You review and bring questions to your next 1:1.
End of weekPatterns from the week's two presentations are summarized for the firm-level review.
